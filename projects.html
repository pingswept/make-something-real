The Senior Design Project

Your team's job is to design, prototype, and build a new tool.

System requirements

  • By "tool", we mean a device that aids a person in fabrication, assembly, simulation, or testing.
  • The tool should not be commercially available for less than $10,000 as of September 1, 2018.
  • The tool can have electrical or software components, but it must have a mechanical component.
  • The tool does not need to be commercially viable, but it should be useful to someone, either a budding engineer such as yourself, or another real person in the world that you can go talk to.

Milestones

  • September 13, 11:45 AM: Narrow down to three preliminary choices.
  • September 20, 11:45 AM: Commit to a tool.
  • September 27, 10:30 AM: Bring a proof of concept to class.
  • October 11, 10:30 AM: Present results of user interviews.
  • October 23, 10:30 AM: Demonstrate a prototype in class.
  • October 30, 10:30 AM: Estimate of specifications due.
  • November 20, 10:30 AM: Final project showcase.
  • November 29, 10:30 AM: Technical report due.

Budget

The Department of Mechanical Engineering will cover up to $100 per student (so $400 for a four-person team) for project expenses.
